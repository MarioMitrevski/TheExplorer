10 Fantastic Things You Have To Do In Rio de Janeiro, Brazil

Sunshine, samba, and sports: these three words are pretty perfect when it comes to describing one of the most popular cities in South America – Rio de Janeiro!

Rio is famed for its beautiful beaches, annual carnival and stunning natural vistas which intertwine with the city so its safe to say, there’s more than enough reason to find yourself wanting to visit! If you’ve already sold on visiting Rio (or indeed if you need a bit more convincing), here are 10 amazing sightings you need to see in Rio de Janeiro!

1.) Christ the Redeemer


Probably the most iconic statue in the world, Christ the Redeemer stands at 98 feet (30 m) tall, looming over the city of Rio at a staggering total height of 2,300 feet (701 m). Talk about a view, right???

Located on the summit of Mount Corcovado, the statue is the result of the combined efforts of three individuals: French sculptor Paul Landowski, Brazilian engineer Heitor da Silva Costa, and French engineer Albert Caquot – kinda in keeping with Rio’s diverse people and cultures.

The statue is accessible by a train that leaves every 30 minutes, from 8:30am to 7:00pm. For the best views, sit on the right side of the train as it makes its way up the mountain. Admission costs 36 Brazilian Real (R$) and the site is open daily.

2.) Sugarloaf Mountain


The 1,300 foot (396 m) tall Sugarloaf Mountain is a rather popular sight to visit in Rio…and one that (understandably) always makes me think food! A two-part cable car trip to the top offers 360 degree views of the city, as well as the surrounding ocean and lush green forests.

Several fast-food restaurants are located on the top of the mountain,so you can grab a bite to eat up there while taking in the gorgeous views. If youâ€™d rather walk, there is a trail that wraps around the mountain.

4.) Samba City Tour


While being in Rio for its famous Carnival would be AMAZING, Samba-season fun can be had year round with a Samba City Tour! The tour has visitors explore the venue where the floats for Carnival are prepared, as well as where the dancers can practice their Samba routines.

Visitors will also get to experience a musical performance, and then be able to try playing instruments used during Carnival. Costume making is also on display, allowing visitors to see the beautiful costumes getting prepped for next year’s Carnival. Tours are every day except for Tuesdays, and are available in English and Portuguese.

5.) Santa Teresa


For a real treat, wander the picturesque streets of Santa Teresa – one of the prettiest neighbourhoods in Rio. The colourful streets are full of charm, and are famed for being particularly artsy.

Be sure to see the Escadaria Selaron, an ongoing staircase art project that uses ceramics, tiles, and mirror pieces from around the world. The Convent is also a top sight in the neighbourhood, as well as the only working streetcar remaining in the city, known as the Bonde de Santa Teresa. 6.) Tijuca Forest


Tijuca Forest is to Rio what Central Park is to New York… except, Tijuca is a tropical rainforest! This 8,300 acre (3,358 hectare) park is home to waterfalls, varying terrain, 1,600 plant species, and over 350 different animal species. Admission is free, and it is open daily from 8:00am to 5:00pm (6:00pm in the winter).
